Gareth Raynor: I'm back from the edge of despair

The winger, 33, revealed how his life spiralled out of control in September when Bradford Bulls sacked him.

The Super League club had handed Raynor a contract after he had served seven months of a 15-month prison sentence for a £36,500 internet scam.

But, after playing 20 games this year, they threw him out ­after discovering he was hooked on sleeping pills.

Raynor said that, on top of worries about paying back £120,000 debts as a result of a fake printer ink business, took him to the edge of despair.

The former Leeds, Hull and ­Crusaders star said: “I had a meltdown.

“I went AWOL from my family for 12 days, staying in hotels and drinking wine, shots and Jack Daniels solidly to try to block out all my problems.

“I’ve been told that the amount I drank could have killed me.

“Then I went home and I know it was stupid but I got a handful of drugs and told my fiancee ­Catriona that I was going to take my life.

“It’s all a bit of a blur now but I do know that Catriona saved my life and got me the help that I needed.”

Raynor started taking sleeping tablets last January because he couldn’t sleep due to worries about his court appearances.

Despite serving a jail term, Raynor still had to pay £120,000 debts or face another sentence.

He said: “Bradford handed me a contract after coming out of prison and I was desperate to do well – but I knew I couldn’t if I wasn’t sleeping properly.

“Before I knew it, I was taking more and more tablets and drinking as well.

“It never affected how I played but team-mates have told me that I’d fall asleep in meetings and I wouldn’t have a clue what we’d done in training.

“I was taking the drugs for the right reasons but it had got out of control and Bradford had to cut their losses. I don’t hold any grudges against them because it was my own fault.”

Catriona appealed to the RFL for help and the couple called in the Sporting Chance clinic.

Raynor said: “The RFL were brilliant. They obviously didn’t want another situation like the one where Terry Newton killed himself.

“Every day for a month, a guy from Sporting Chance visited me and helped me so much that it was decided I didn’t need to be admitted to their clinic.

“For the next month, he visited me four days a week and now we’re down to one day and I’m doing really well.”

Raynor admits he used to ­bottle up his problems but is speaking out, in the week that Gary Speed killed himself, in the hope that it will help ­others.

Thefather-of-twoadded: “Sometimesnegativescan have a positive effect on other people, by showing that help is out there.

“I’m now off all the tablets, haven’t touched a drop of drink since September and I’m ­training hard again and hoping to get back playing.”

Raynor admits he has little chance of signing for a Super League club again.

He said: “The thing I’d love to do is play for Featherstone in the Championship. I grew up supporting them.

“This is not out of desperation because I really don’t think I’ve anything to prove. But after the last few dramatic years I’ve had I’d just like to get back to the ­enjoyment of playing.”
