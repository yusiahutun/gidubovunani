Details of WWE superstars' salaries as John Cena tops list of highest earning wrestlers

- John Cena currently tops the list of highest earning WWE superstars based on their base salaries

- The 16-time champion also ventured into acting in recent years

- Most wrestlers, aside from their salary, earn millions from appearances in the main events and merchandise sales

The WWE is home to some of the most popular superstars in the world, and for good reason as well.

These celebrities not only put their bodies on the line for our entertainment but also make a handsome amount of money doing so.

Majority of your favorite wrestlers earn a staggering amount of money, usually seven-figure, while the bigger names also reap from bonuses for main event appearances and merchandise sales.

In 2019, Brock Lesnar was right up there in the list of highest earning wrestlers and not much has changed a year later.

His contract with WWE might be running down but The Beast Incarnate is still very secure financially as he made an astonishing $12million-a-year (KSh 1.3 billion) and an addition KSh 55 million per main event appearance.

Second on the list of high-earning wrestlers at WWE is expectedly John Cena, who made KSh 933 million in the past year.

Cena, a 16-time world champion, is also making a name for himself in Holywood as an actor.

Roman Reigns comes third on the list as he made KSh 570 million in base earnings this past year.

Randy Orton follows closely with KSh 511 million and he is followed by the likes of AJ Styles and Seth Rollins on KSh 394 million and KSh 336 million respectively.

The Undertaker, who recently announced it was his time to retire, made KSh 365 million.
