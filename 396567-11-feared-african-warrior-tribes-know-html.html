11 most feared African warrior tribes you need to know

Which are the most feared African warrior tribes? Throughout the history of ancient Africa, various kingdoms held battles and wars against each other to gain dominance. Amongst the greatest warrior tribes in the continent, some have stood out because of their unique strategies when fighting in a war, the weapons they use, or their leaders.

In ancient African history, the flourishing tribes in the continent largely depended on their warriors for expansion and protection. Warrior tribes with a strong military were feared by the smaller ones, and they maintained their dominance for centuries. As a process of survival, many tribes focused on ensuring that their warriors had the best weapons and training.

List of famous African warrior tribes

Which tribe is known as the warrior tribe of Africa? Who were the greatest African warriors? These are some of the most common questions that people ask about the African warrior tribes. Here is a list of warring tribes in Africa:

1. Somalis

What is the most dangerous tribe in Africa? Top on the list of deadliest African warriors is the Somali tribe. The Somali are undoubtedly the most powerful of all African tribes when it comes to military warfare and tactics. They boast of the best military warfare and tactics which helped them to sail as far as Southeast Asia to flex their power.

The history of their military skills and prowess dates back to the Land of Punt, where they sent their powerful mercenaries to Egypt and defeated the ancient Hittites. The Somalis were the first ancient African tribe to get involved in naval warfare. During the medieval period, they were the first to adopt modern weaponry and used them to conquer and colonize Abyssinia.

2. Nubians

Second on the list of great African warriors is the Nubian tribe. The military tactics and prowess of the Nubian tribe allowed them to conquer the surrounding kingdoms and to establish the Kush Kingdom, which is one of the longest-lasting kingdoms in the continent.

The Kush Kingdom possessed the ability to resist and fight invaders from the Middle East. It became very prosperous and powerful as it conquered Egypt. The Nubian war tribe is also famous for successfully defeating and resisting the Arab Caliphate.

3. Abyssinians

The Abyssinian tribe is third on the list of African tribal warriors. It boasts of some of the best warfare skills in African ancient history. The history of this warrior tribe dates back to the Axum period. During this period, they conquered and then colonized the Southern Arabians.

They lived in a mountainous environment, and they developed their military skills to resist any form of invasion in the area. Abyssinians enjoy political and cultural dominance in Eritrea and Ethiopia.

4. Zulus

The Zulu tribe is the most famous of all the warrior tribes in the African continent. They are the largest tribe in Southern Africa. The Zulus possessed the ability to calculate excellent military strategies. The Zulu tribe had a large army, but their success was based on their ability to take over other tribes and absorb them rather instead of destroying them.

During the colonial era, the Zulus gave Britain a run for their money even though they did not possess advance weapons. This proves how strong and powerful the Zulu tribe was in terms of military tactics. The fearsome reputation created by the Zulus is still acknowledged to this day.

5. Berbers

The Berber tribe is top on the list of West African warriors. This warrior tribe is most well-known for conquering Spain, defeating the Roman Empire attempt at invasion, and resisting against the colonizers from ancient Persia.

The Berbers had very powerful armies, and they expanded their conquered territories to include many of the kingdoms in West Africa and converting them to Islam. They are also famous for maintaining a dominant presence of the Mediterranean Sea.

6. Oromos

The Oromo tribe is one of the largest groups in the East African community. They are renowned world over for their brutality. During the 16th century, the Oromos expanded their territory in the Horn of Africa and took the most fertile lands in the region.

7. Mali people

The Mali people are renowned for establishing one of the most powerful empires in the West African region. This tribe boasted of the most powerful military capability that helped them to conquer, colonize, and unite many of the West African tribes.

They resisted the Portuguese invaders and forced the Berbers of North Africa to pay tribute to their leaders.

8. Maasai

Possibly the most famous of all African tribes, the Maasai are some of the fiercest warriors in the continent. They live along the semi-arid Great Rift Valley in Kenya and Tanzania. The Maasai are warriors who trace their ancestry to the northern Great Rift Valley in Sudan.

9. Fulani

The list of Nigerian warriors can never be complete without the mention of Fulani. Fulani is the largest nomadic tribe in West Africa. Although they were primarily herders and traders, they developed their military expertise with time. They are famous for being fierce nomadic warriors that subjected and enslaved their fellow West Africans.

10. Shona

Shona tribe is the largest indigenous group in Zimbabwe. The group is famous for establishing the great Zimbabwe Empire in South Africa. They took over other cultures and tribes, including the Swahilis, making them pay tribute to the rulers of Shona

11. Amazons of Dahomey

The Amazons of Dahomey are the most famous African woman warrior tribes. The women had extraordinary abilities as they hunted and fought for their tribe. The King of Dahomey was very impressed with their military ability. He gathered the women together and made a tribe of warrior women who became the most invincible tribes at the time.
