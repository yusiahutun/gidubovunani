The Downton Abbey actress squeezed her feet into nude peep-toe heels to attend the WGSN Global Fashion Awards at the Savoy Hotel

As the old saying goes, if the shoe fits, wear it.

And if it doesn’t fit but it goes with your outfit? Well, wear it anyway. 

Downton Abbey actress Joanne Froggatt was so determined to get off on the right foot at a fashion event that she wedged her feet into a pair of nude stilettos - which opened at the front to reveal her painfully squashed toes.

Miss Froggatt, 32, teamed the uncomfortable-looking Nicholas Kirkwood footwear with a simple bright dress for the WGSN Global Fashion Awards dinner at The Savoy.

Her appearance came just weeks after she walked up the aisle with IT director James Cannon – hopefully in more comfortable footwear.

The star, who plays head housemaid Anna Bates in the ITV drama series, is now taking time off to enjoy married life.

She said: ‘I’m just enjoying it at the moment. I keep turning down work but I should probably stop doing that soon.’

The awards ceremony was hosted by newlywed TV presenter Cat Deeley, who recently married Irish comedian Patrick Kielty in secret.

Other stars to hit the red carpet at the London hotel included singers Pixie Lott and VV Brown, presenter Zara Martin and actor Luke Evans.

The awards recognise and reward those who push the boundaries of fashion globally.

Categories include store design, visual merchandising, ethical practice and marketing.

The third series of Downton Abbey came to an end on Sunday night, with a whopping 10.7million viewers tuning in to watch the gripping finale, which ended on a happy note for much of the cast.

The resounding success of the most-watched episode of the series helped make Downton the highest rating drama of the year, according to ITV.

The period drama, which also counts Dame Maggie Smith and Hugh Bonneville among its stellar cast, had  2million more viewers than BBC drama Call The Midwife and 2.7million more than Sherlock.

The episode saw newcomer Lady Rose, played by 23-year-old actress Lily James run away to embark upon an affair with a married man in London, only to be discovered in the Blue Dragon nightclub by her disapproving relatives.

Alongside the high profile role in the closing episode of Downton, the Guildhall School of Music and Drama-trained actress is set to appear alongside Hollywood heavyweights Liam Neeson and Ralph Fiennes in the film Wrath of the Titans.
