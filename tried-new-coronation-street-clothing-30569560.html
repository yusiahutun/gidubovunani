I tried the new Coronation Street clothing collection and one thing left me gobsmacked

When I heard that one of my favourite fashion brands was partnering with ITV soap Coronation Street to release an exclusive clothing collection, I obviously just had to try it out.

I mean, who wouldn’t want to rep the iconic supermarket chain Freshcos or everyone’s favourite sandwich shop Roy’s Rolls?

That’s exactly what Joanie have gone for. As well as a stunning 50s style dress emblazoned with some of the most iconic buildings on the cobbles, you can sport branded items of some of the most famous fictional companies.

So as soon as my order arrived in the post, I was desperate to try it all on. I opted for the McDonald Coronation Street print dress – a green, knee-length piece featuring illustrations of Corrie houses, Dev’s corner shop, The Kabin and the Rovers Return.

Also in my package was the Tatlock Red Brick Print Cardigan – based, of course, on Weatherfield’s iconic red brick streets – and the Holdsworth Freshco pale blue sweatshirt.

But you can also get your hands on a green Newton and Ridley Bitter sweatshirt to rep the fictional alcohol brand, a creamy Roy’s Rolls t-shirt, and an oversized shirt version of the green dress.

When I was all dressed, it was time to find somewhere to show off my new threads. And where better than the seaside town of Whitby – home to Goths, Dracula and… apparently quite a few Corrie fans.

Honestly, when heading out, I didn’t expect anyone to recognise what I was wearing, especially as the dress’s print is so subtle! As opposed to Primark’s Greggs merch, it doesn’t have the words Coronation Street pasted over the front.

Which made it even more special when I connected with other soap fans. While browsing a bookshop, one staff member shyly approached me to ask: “Is that Coronation Street on your dress?”

I was gobsmacked.

It was such a sweet moment – and made all the more perfect knowing that I wasn’t cringing in a set of merch that brazenly advertised whatever fandom I’m currently repping.

Another thing about this collection is the quality. The sizing is perfect, the buttons on the dress don’t gape, and all the material is so, so creamy.

Also, the dress has pockets. Pockets!

The redbrick cardigan is another of my favourites, with buttons down the front and a really cosy feel to it – perfect for an autumn evening before you huddle down to watch the soaps on TV.

And the attention to detail is really something else. Joanie Brand Director Lucy explained that she worked with ITV producers to “ensure the devil was in the detail”.

That included “making sure the window lintels to the famous terrace were perfectly accurate, matching the window graphics in Dev’s corner shop to ensure continuity [and] making sure Norris’ paperboy was standing outside the Kabin”.

All of the six pieces in the collection are made with responsible fabrics, and honestly, you can tell.

Finally, a long day of shopping done, it was time to settle down, and I couldn’t think of a better way to finish my Corrie-themed day than sipping on a gin cocktail using the official Coronation Street gin, Wonky Duck.

The No.186 gin was crafted in tribute to the legend that is Hilda Ogden, with Corrie partnering with the Manchester distillery to craft the liquor.

Hilda’s wonky duck mural is considered a staple of the show, so it made perfect sense to name the gin after it. It’s even Eccles Cake flavoured.

My advice? Mix the gin with diet lemonade. It really brings out the fruity flavours without being overpowered by the fizz. Wonky Duck made the perfect summer drink to end the day on.

Am I living in Weatherfield right now?
