Mark Hamill is an American actor, voice actor, and writer best known for his portrayal of Luke Skywalker in the Star Wars film series. His performance garnered him several honors, including three Saturn Awards.

In addition, Hamill is well-known in DC Comics for his long-running roles as “The Joker” in “Batman: The Animated Series” (1992-1994), “Fire Lord Ozai in Avatar: The Last Airbender” (2005-2008), and “Skips in Regular Show” (2010-2017).

Where was Mark Hamill born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Mark Hamill was born in Oakland, California, in the United States, on September 25, 1951. Mark Richard Hamill is his given name. He is a citizen of the United States. Hamill is of White descent and was born under the astrological sign of Libra.

Mark Hamill is the seventh of seven children born to Virginia Suzanne, a half-Swedish and half-English mother, and William Thomas Hamill, who hailed from England, Scotland, Wales, and Ireland. William, his father, was a Captain in the United States Navy, and Virginia, his mother, was a stay-at-home parent with seven children.

Mark was raised in a family of six siblings, including two brothers, Will and Patrick, and four sisters, Terry, Jan, Jeanie, and Kim. Because of his father’s job, he and his siblings had to change schools frequently.

He attended Walsingham Academy and Poe Middle School before transferring to Hale Junior High School in San Diego. His family moved to Virginia shortly after he started at James Madison High School, so he transferred to Annandale High School.

His father had already been stationed in Japan during his junior year, and Hamill attended Nile C. Kinnick High School, where he was a member of the Drama Club and graduated in 1969. His education was furthered at Los Angeles City College, where he majored in acting.

He started his career right after college with a recurring role on the serial drama “General Hospital,” and went on to participate in several television films, including “The City” and “Sarah T. – Portrait of a Teenage Alcoholic.”

How did Mark start his Professional Career?

Hamill’s professional career began as a lead in the brief-lived comedy “The Texas Wheelers.”

In 1977, Hamill got his big break when he was cast as Luke Skywalker in the blockbuster “Star Wars.” The picture was a huge success and is now considered one of the most influential works in movie history.

He later appeared in the adventure comedy film “Corvette Summer” in 1978. In 1980, he played Luke Skywalker again in the second Star Wars film trilogy, “The Empire Strikes Back.”

He played the same character in “Return of the Jedi” three years later, in 1983. In “Star Wars: The Last Jedi,” which was released on December 15, 2017, Hamill resumed his role as Luke Skywalker.

After a six-year hiatus, Hamill returned to cinema in 1989 with “Slipstream,” a science fiction feature.

He also acted in the films “Midnight Ride” (1990), “The Guyver” (1991), “Timerunner” (1993), and “Hamilton” (1998).

He played the antagonist Cocknocker in the 2001 film “Jay and Silent Bob Strike Back.”
