The Fun Story Behind Pouring Gatorade on Coaches at the Super Bowl How Did This Wacky Tradition

 Posted on April 14, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  592 words  |  Larita Shotwell

The Super Bowl, America’s most anticipated sporting event, brings with it not just a battle for the coveted championship but also unique traditions that have become an integral part of its legacy. One such tradition is the victorious team dousing their head coach in Gatorade, a ritual that has transcended its original prank status to become a symbol of triumph at the Super Bowl.

A Splash from the Past: The Origins of the Gatorade Shower

The Gatorade shower, a gesture of celebration and respect, traces its roots back to a chilly day in 1986. The New York Giants, having just clinched a victory against the then-Washington Redskins in the NFC Championship game, decided to give their coach, Bill Parcells, a wet and wild surprise. Jim Burt, the team’s nose tackle, along with his teammates, grabbed a cooler of Gatorade and poured it over Parcells’ head, sparking what would become one of the most iconic traditions in sports.

The tradition was cemented in Super Bowl lore when the Giants carried their celebratory antics into their victory against the Denver Broncos, setting a precedent for future champions. Since then, the Gatorade shower has evolved into a much-anticipated moment of the Super Bowl, with fans and players alike speculating not if but when and in what color the shower will occur.

The Color of Victory: Gatorade Showers at the Super Bowl

The color of the Gatorade has become a subject of bets and speculation. For instance, when the Kansas City Chiefs emerged victorious against the Philadelphia Eagles in Super Bowl 2023, head coach Andy Reid was bathed in purple Gatorade, a choice that came with +1000 odds. This moment of purple rain was not just a celebration but also a reminder of how deeply ingrained this tradition has become in the fabric of the Super Bowl.

As we look forward to Super Bowl 2024, with the Chiefs set to face the 49ers in a highly anticipated rematch of their 2020 showdown, speculation about the color of the Gatorade has already begun. Historically, orange has been a favorite, used five times since 2001.

Yet, intriguingly, red or pink has never made an appearance in the 21st-century Super Bowl Gatorade celebrations. The odds from DraftKings paint a vivid picture of the bets being placed, with red leading the speculation, followed by orange, and the elusive “No Gatorade Bath” option lingering as an outlier with +1600 odds.

The Significance Behind the Splash

The Gatorade shower is more than just a playful tradition; it is a moment of unity and joy. It symbolizes the culmination of a season’s hard work, dedication, and the overcoming of challenges. For the coaches, it’s a wet acknowledgment of their leadership and strategy. For the players, it’s a moment of shared triumph. And for the fans, it’s a colorful spectacle that marks the zenith of the Super Bowl experience.

Conclusion: Embracing Tradition with Every Drop

As we gear up for another Super Bowl, the anticipation for the game is matched by the curiosity surrounding the Gatorade shower. This tradition, born out of a spontaneous moment of jubilation, has become a cherished part of Super Bowl history. It reminds us that victory can come in many colors, each symbolizing the unpredictable and exhilarating nature of football. Whether it’s red, orange, or perhaps for the first time, pink, the Gatorade shower will continue to be a vibrant testament to the spirit of the Super Bowl, drenching the winning coach in the sweet hue of success.
