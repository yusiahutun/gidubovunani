Bald Vince Vaughn rocks wig on The Ellen DeGeneres Show

He shaved off all his hair for a brand new role.

And Vince Vaughn's dramatic new look hasn't gone unnoticed by his five-year-old daughter, Locklyn.

The bald actor, 46, admitted during an appearance for Tuesday's episode of The Ellen DeGeneres Show that he's faced a bit of teasing from his little girl ever since shaving off his hair.

The actor's representative told Entertainment Tonight that Vince's new hairdo was actually for his role of Bradley Thomas in the film Brawl In Cell Block 99.

Vince had arrived to the show to promote his new film Hacksaw Ridge, which is about Pfc. Desmond T. Doss, a real-life hero and conscientious objector who won the Congressional Medal of Honor despite refusing to bear arms during WWII on religious grounds.

The actor, who plays Sergeant Howell in the film, admitted to utilizing old acting techniques as he had to raise his voice often for his role. 

'I had to really be conscious of like, the old acting stuff of going to your diaphragm and really supporting it, but there was moments, because the battles were so intense, we shot 'em all practical,' he said.

Vince stepped out on stage wearing a black wig, which he jokingly insisted was his real hair.

The actor's bald head was unveiled in full after Ellen reached over to run her fingers through the wig, in spite of his initial objections.
