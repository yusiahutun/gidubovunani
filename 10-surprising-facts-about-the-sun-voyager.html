10 Surprising Facts About The Sun Voyager

The Sun Voyager is not a Viking ship.

Contrary to popular belief, The Sun Voyager is not actually a Viking ship. It is a modern art sculpture resembling a dream boat, designed by Icelandic artist Jón Gunnar Árnason.

It is located in Reykjavik, Iceland.

The Sun Voyager stands proudly along the waterfront of Reykjavik, the capital city of Iceland. It has become one of the most recognizable landmarks in the city.

The sculpture symbolizes a voyage of discovery.

Árnason designed The Sun Voyager as an ode to the sun and a symbol of hope, progress, and the human spirit’s eternal search for new horizons. It represents the ever-continuing journey of the Icelandic people.

It is made entirely of stainless steel.

The Sun Voyager is constructed entirely of stainless steel, giving it a sleek and reflective appearance. It stands at an impressive 17 meters long and 9 meters high.

It was unveiled in 1990 to celebrate Reykjavik’s 200th anniversary.

The Sun Voyager was officially unveiled on August 18, 1990, as a part of the celebrations for Reykjavik’s 200th anniversary. It has since become an important cultural symbol for the city.

The sculpture faces towards the Atlantic Ocean.

Positioned to face the Atlantic Ocean, The Sun Voyager offers a stunning view of the surrounding waters and the majestic Mount Esja in the distance.

The Sun Voyager has been featured in numerous films and TV shows.

Thanks to its unique and captivating design, The Sun Voyager has caught the attention of filmmakers and has appeared in several movies and TV shows, adding to its fame and allure.

The artist’s vision was inspired by Icelandic heritage and mythology.

Jón Gunnar Árnason drew inspiration from the rich Icelandic heritage and mythology for his creation of The Sun Voyager. The sculpture is said to embody the spirit of the Norse seafarers who first settled in Iceland.

The Sun Voyager has become a popular tourist attraction.

Due to its striking appearance and symbolic significance, The Sun Voyager has become a must-visit landmark for tourists exploring Reykjavik. It is often crowded with visitors capturing its beauty through photographs.

The sculpture is lit up at night.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the Sun Voyager is not just a striking sculpture, but also a symbol of hope, exploration, and cultural heritage. FAQs

1. What is the significance of the Sun Voyager?

The Sun Voyager symbolizes hope, freedom, and progress. It represents the spirit of exploration and discovery and serves as a tribute to Iceland’s rich history and Viking heritage.

2. Who created the Sun Voyager?

The Sun Voyager was created by Icelandic sculptor Jon Gunnar Arnason. It was his vision to capture the essence of a Viking ship, sailing towards the unknown and embodying the spirit of adventure.

3. Is the Sun Voyager made of bronze?

Yes, the Sun Voyager is made entirely of stainless steel, giving it a sleek and modern appearance. Its metallic surface reflects the sunlight during the day, creating a captivating visual effect.

4. Can I climb on the Sun Voyager?

No, climbing on the Sun Voyager is not permitted. It is a piece of artwork intended for viewing and appreciation from a distance. There are barriers in place to ensure the preservation and safety of the sculpture.

5. Is there an entrance fee to see the Sun Voyager?

No, there is no entrance fee to see the Sun Voyager. It is located in a public space and can be visited free of charge at any time of the day.
