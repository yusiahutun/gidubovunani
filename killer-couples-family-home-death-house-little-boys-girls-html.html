Killer couples: How the family home became a death house for little boys and girls

Before she was murdered Kiesha Abrahams was in a situation which few people, let alone a six-year-old child, could hope to survive.

Every time Kiesha turned her blue eyes in a face framed with blonde curls up to Kristi Ann Abrahams, her mother would become enraged over the little girl's resemblance to her biological father and hated ex, Chris Weippeart. 

When Kiesha, from Sydney, was just 15 months old, Kristi had bitten her on the shoulder. Aged three, the girl told a social worker her mother had burned her with a cigarette.

When Kristi acquired a new boyfriend, Robert Smith - who is due to walk from prison this month, Kiesha's days were numbered.

According to Australian research on child murders, a 'stepfather' can be particularly dangerous in an already volatile situation.

'The de facto male kills in a particularly violent way,' said Dr Lilian De Bortoli, a forensic behavioural scientist who now works as an adviser at Court Services Victoria.

Dr Bortoli, who researched  filicide - the act of a parent, or parent equivalent killing their child - said among 155 child murders she had studied, de facto fathers tended to inflict 'multiple abdominal injuries, and head and spinal injuries, and has usually been abusing the child for some time before he kills them'. 

Kristi Abrahams (above with Kiesha and de facto Robert Smith) bit, beat and burned her daughter before killing her at the age of six. Smith will be released this month around the time of what would have been Kiesha's 19th birthday

About 10 per cent of homicide victims in Australia are children aged 0–17 years, and the majority are victims of filicide , according to a 2019 Australian Institute of Criminology study, Filicide Offenders.

International and Australian research indicates that children aged under five years make up the largest proportion of filicide victims, but some of Australia's worst cases are older. 

Six-year-old John Ashfield, from the NSW town of East Nowra, died when his mother struck him with a metal curtain rod and his stepfather forced him into his sister's dress and took to him with a hammer as the little boy begged and repeated, 'I'm sorry'. 

Those and the case of 'Ebony', the seven-year-old NSW girl who was starved by her parents and left lying in her own faeces in a locked room, are no less shocking for the fact the children had made it to first grade.

The majority of children including Tyrell Cobb were killed by a stepfather when they were aged between one and four years old. 

Heidi Strbak (above with son Tyrell Cobb) served three years for killing him in a brutal assault which she has always denied, saying she had no hand in the attack

Laughing killer Matthew Scown caused outrage when captured walking free after serving just three years for the manslaughter of Tyrell Cobb

A post-mortem revealed the four-year-old Gold Coast boy had 53 bruises and 17 abrasions from head to legs and while his mother was convicted, his stepfather Matthew Scown caused national outrage when he walked from prison on a plea deal.

Scown, convicted of manslaughter, was released after three years for time served and was captured on camera laughing at his own luck. 

Like many male child killers, Robert Smith will be released before the mother, after serving 10 years of a maximum 16 year sentence.

His release from April 21, a day before what would have been Kiesha's 19th birthday, sparked anger and a protest outside court following a decision by the NSW State Parole Authority. 

These are the children who became the victims of couples who kill, their 'safe' family homes turned into places of terror and death. 

KIESHA ABRAHAMS 

Kristi Abrahams cries crocodile tears while pretending her daughter had gone missing when in reality she had killed Kiesha

Victim: Six-year-old Kiesha Weippeart, aka Kiesha Abrahams.

Killers: Her biological mother, Kristi Ann Abrahams, and Abrahams' de facto, Robert Smith.

Death: In July 2010 after her mother knocked her unconscious for refusing to put on her pyjamas.

Prison sentences: In 2013, Kristi Abrahams was jailed for murder for a minimum of 16 years and maximum of 22 years. Smith was jailed for a maximum of 12 years for manslaughter and accessory to murder.

The crime: After inflicting serious head injuries on Kiesha who was then showered and 'put to bed' where she died, Kristi Abrahams and Robert Smith hid the little girl's body in a suitcase inside a wardrobe for five days before dumping it in bushland at Shalvey, in Sydney's outer west.

The pair poured petrol over Keisha's body and set it alight, before burying her in a shallow grave dug with a hammer, and returning home to report her as missing.

Kiesha was progressively beaten by her mother and then murdered, her body put in  a suitcase and set on fire then placed in a shallow grave in bushland 

The ruse: Kristi Abrahams shed tears for media cameras alongside Robert Smith, saying they had no idea who could have taken Kiesha while imploring 'please contact the police, she's beautiful'.

Neighbours and friends searched the streets near Mt Druitt with the couple keeping up the pretence until April 2011, when police found Kiesha's skeletal remains in the charred suitcase and charged the pair.

Kiesha's body bore injuries consistent with long-term abuse and serious blunt force trauma suggesting that she had been slammed into the ground or another hard object.

JOHN ASHFIELD

Victim: John Ashfield, 6. 

Killers: His biological mother, Gunn-Britt Ashfield, and her de facto Austin Allan Hughes - now known as Angelic Karstrom and Blain Lopez Smith.

Death: In 1993 in East Nowra when Ashfield, 25, and Hughes, 20, became enraged after it was suggested John had behaved 'inappropriately' with a younger sister.

Prison sentences:  Ashfield was jailed for a maximum of 19 years and released after 18 years in 2011, under the name of Angelic Karstrom. Hughes walked from Silverwater prison in 2009 after serving 16 years.

John Ashfield, 6, pleaded as he was beaten with a hammer, saying over and over 'I'm sorry, I'm sorry' but succumbed to the attack by his mother, Angelic Karstrom, and her de facto, Blain Lopez Smith

The crime: Committed in front of John's four siblings, the murder began as a fight after school with Ashfield and Hughes targeting John, punching him and hitting with with a metal curtain rod.

They had decided to teach John 'a lesson' and, as his siblings watched or listened, the beating turned into a frenzy over more than two hours.

The little boy sobbed, begged them to stop, repeating 'I'm sorry' only to be told by Hughes 'you scream like a little girl,' and forcing him to wear one of his sister's dresses.

Austin Allan Hughes, now known as Blain Lopez Smith, served 16 years in prison for for the brutal hammer slaying of six-year-old John Ashfield in 1993

Gunn-Britt Ashfield (above) was 25 when she and her de facto targeted her first grader son John  in a brutal attack with a hammer while the little boy cried 'I'm sorry, I'm sorry'

John cried, 'Get it off, I'm not a girl' and an enraged Hughes grabbed a hammer, and with a phone book against John's head, repeatedly beat the book until John flopped back on the bed.

Ashfield put her unconscious son under a cold shower, then a hot shower, then called an ambulance.

The ruse: The couple coached John's eight-year-old brother, Allan, to say teenage youths had set upon them as they returned home from buying milk.

John Ashfield suffered retinal haemorrhage and extensive bruising to his whole body, and died in hospital the next day.

TYRELL COBB

Victim: Tyrell Cobb, 4.

Killers: His biological mother, Heidi Strbak and her de facto, Matthew Scown.

Death: In May 2009, from blunt force trauma to the stomach sustained at the Biggera Waters home on the Gold Coast home where he lived with Strbak and Scown.

Prison sentences:  Strbak pleaded guilty in 2017 to manslaughter for failing to seek treatment for her son's injuries. She was sentenced to nine years but released in 2020 after her term was reduced on appeal to five years maximum. She has always denied she caused the injuries.

In 2022 she suffered head injuries in a fall, went into a coma, lost her unborn baby  and her life support was switched off, but she survived and was released to her family who continue to campaign to prove her innocence as they care for her.

Tyrell Cobb was rushed to a Gold Coast hospital covered in 70 injuries, including bruises on his ear, and two blows to his abdomen that caused his stomach contents to leak

Heidi Strbak served three years in prison, then collapsed at home and was taken off life support but survived and is now cared for by her family who continue to defend her innocence

Matthew Scown also claimed not to have delivered the fatal injuries to Tyrell. He was convicted of manslaughter and released from jail in 2017 after serving three years of a four-year sentence. He infamously laughed in front of media cameras upon his release.

The crime: The couple had been together for less than a year on the Gold Coast when Tyrell died in May 2009 from internal bleeding and abdominal injuries caused by blunt force trauma. 

Tyrell was rushed to a Gold Coast hospital covered in 70 injuries, including bruises on his ear, and two blows to his abdomen that caused his stomach contents to leak and internal bleeding.

The boy died a painful death after continuously vomiting green bile. A post-mortem revealed he had 53 bruises and 17 abrasions from his head to his legs when he died.

EBONY

Victim: Seven-year-old 'Ebony'

Killers: Her biological parents, SW and BW

Death: In November 2007, at the Hawks Nest home of her parents where the body of Ebony, 7, weighing just 9kg was found lying in a squalid room covered in her own waste

Prison sentences:  The mother, SW, was jailed for life for murder but it was reduced to a maximum of 40 years on appeal, with the father BW convicted of manslaughter, with a maximum of 16 years and was released after 15 in early 2022.

The crime: Suffering from autism, Ebony was the youngest of three sisters living with their Valium-addicted mother and her controlling husband in Sydney before they moved to Hawks Nest, where the child was imprisoned in a bedroom which a judge would say also 'became her toilet'.

Ebony's parents starved her to death, with her 9kg body being found in a  filthy room lying in her own waste, aged 7

Ebony's father BW was a controlling prescription drug addict who blamed his wife for Ebony's death and served only 15 years whereas SW received life reduced to 40 years on appeal

The ruse: Both parents claimed Ebony was moving around and eating before her death, but medical evidence said Ebony was so physically deteriorated she would most likely have been unable to stand, sit or swallow food for days before her death.

JOSEPH 

Victim: 'Joseph', 3.

Killers: His biological mother, LN, and stepfather, AW.

Death: In August 2014, the boy was taken to hospital and placed in the Intensive Care Unit but did not regain consciousness and his life support was switched off.

Prison sentences:  In 2017, the NSW Supreme Court sentenced Joseph's mother to a minimum 33 years and her de facto to 30 years for murder, with the judge saying they were 'de facto life sentences', for their 'grotesque and cruel' crime. The mother will be eligible for parole when she is 73 years old.

The crime: Just as Kristi Abrahams resented her daughter Kiesha because of her resemblance to her biological father, Joseph's mother hated him because 'he looked like his father'.

Joseph was three when he went to live with his mother and her de facto.  He became the victim of cruelty and torture over 51 days inside a house at Oberon

LN had gone to live with her de facto AW in a modest fibro house in Oberon, west of Sydney, where in the winter of 2014, pain, misery and terrible violence was being inflicted on little Joseph.

Then aged 39, LN would later agree with police that she wanted to kill Joseph, saying 'Don't get me wrong I did love that boy but there was part of me that hated him. Because he looked like his father'.

For 51 days, Joseph suffered a cruelty and torture. He was beaten, locked inside an Esky full of ice to bring out his bruises, had his head rammed into a cupboard door and had a ball taped into his mouth..

Joseph's stepfather AW is interrogated by police as to why he locked the little boy in an Esky full of ice and subjected the three-year-old to cruelty and torture

Joseph's mother admitted she hated her son Joseph and wanted to kill him, torturing the 3-year-old over 51 days before he succumbed to the cruel brutality and died in hospital

Finally, on Sunday August 2, 2014, the couple called the police. In hospital, Joseph's other relatives soon realised the damaged little boy in the ICU bed was not the happy and healthy toddler taken from them seven weeks earlier.

The woman said her young son had 'just pushed my buttons too many times' and that she had 'so many thoughts about killing Joseph' before she did.

Justice Peter Johnson said Joseph's end came when he had no real capacity to defend himself and that the boy had been 'worn down physically and psychologically' by the torture.

The ruse:  The couple told emergency services that Joseph had been rendered unconscious after tripping over dog leads on the oval across the street.
