Jessica Chastain reveals someone in Hollywood 'spanked her'

That Hollywood is a sexist industry is certainly not news, but Jessica Chastain is the latest actress to comment on her own personal experience of it, confessing that in the early days of her career she experienced it regularly and that one particular incident remains clear in her memory. "I won't say who, but I've been on a movie where someone very important… I had been walking down the hall and they kind of spanked me on the butt. And I did turn around and say, 'Did you just spank me?' I was really upset about it. But in their mind it was completely normal. It was fine behaviour," she told The Telegraph.

"I think stuff like that happens all the time," she added. "Probably now it would never happen to me because I'm really… I'm not a shrinking violet. If I see behaviour against me, or against anyone, that is unjust, then I will absolutely say something."

The actress may claim to be older and wiser, but she won't reveal her actual age: "I never talk about my age. Honestly, in male profiles, they never talk about the age. And I feel that the media needs to treat women the same as they treat men, and not perpetuate the problem." It is widely know that Chastain was in her mid-30s when she began her acting career.

In her latest film, Miss Sloane, Jessica Chastain stars as the eponymous lead character Elizabeth Sloane, a tough Washington lobbyist who takes on the political establishment. From her don't-mess-with-me attitude, it sounds to us like she's perfect for the role.
