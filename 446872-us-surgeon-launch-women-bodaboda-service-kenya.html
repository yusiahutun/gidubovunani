US Surgeon to Launch Women-Only Boda Boda Service in Kenya

  • Rhiana Menen, a volunteer doctor from the US, said they would launch a program to equip women with boda bodas
  • Menen said the program was conceived after learning that boda boda riders were taking advantage of school girls
  • The Boda Girls will receive the bikes through a lease, and they will also be involved in offering free transport to hospitals

A Kenyan-based surgeon from the US has announced plans to launch a women-only boda boda service in the country.

Boda boda riders taking advantage of girls

Dr Rhiana Menen from the Tiba Foundation said that she got the idea for the initiative after learning that boda boda riders were harassing young girls.

Menen said they had gone to distribute menstrual kits at a school in Ugenya, Siaya county, and after the session, two girls hopped on the back of a boda boda and rode away.

“The principal told us the girls are vulnerable as they get taken to fields by the riders and assaulted," she told Boise State Public Radio.

After consulting the women in the area, they realised that bodabodas could also play a key role in helping women earn a steady income.

The program’s pilot, which will be launched in April, will involve 12 women aged between 16 and 30.

Roles of Boda Girls

The Boda Girls will be responsible for giving reproductive health lessons to schools, distributing menstrual kits and encouraging girls to stay in school.

“They will also give about 5,000 free rides to the hospital,” she disclosed.

Every Boda Girl and her family will also get free insurance for themselves and their families, and they will also be trained as community health workers.

The motorbikes will be given to them through a lease agreement at a minimal price with no interest.

With earnings from around KSh 400 a day, the Boda Girls will then be expected to buy their motorbikes at the end of the year.
