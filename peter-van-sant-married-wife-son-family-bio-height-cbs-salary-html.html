Career and Progression:

Peter Van Sant started his journalistic career from KAPY-LP in Port Angeles, WA. He then worked for KMTV-TV in Twin Falls, Cedar-Rapids based KCRG-TV and Omaha-based KETV. In 1978, he entered Fox-owned KOOL-TV as weekend anchor and reporter in Phoenix, Arizona and moved to WFAA-TV in Dallas in 1982.

Similarly, he joined CBS News in 1984 and worked in Atlanta as a correspondent for CBS Evening News. During the time, his investigative reporting on a high number of medical helicopter crashes helped him receive 1986 Emmy Award. He became the first journalist to report on the devastating famine in North Korea and won the third Emmy Award.

Some of the major national events he reported include Hurricane Katrina, Virginia Tech massacre and September 11 Attacks. He has also co-authored a book named ‘Perfectly Executed,' and also wrote the script and produced the documentary titled, ‘Three Days in September.'
