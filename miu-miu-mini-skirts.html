Miu Miu's mini skirts will take you right back

The spring/summer 2022 shows have officially wrapped – and we have seen some history-making moments, including a Versace x Fendi collaboration and a fashion first when Balenciaga teamed up with The Simpsons on a very special episode. But, the return of the runway also brought some definitive news: there is no escaping Nineties and Noughties nostalgia next season.

On the final day of Paris Fashion Week, Chanel hosted a Nineties-inspired catwalk show, which featured the traditional runway format, complete with photographers leaning on the raised platform. It was an ode to Karl Lagerfeld's supermodel-studded catwalk shows of the decade – and the collection tapped right into the mood of today, whereby customers are seeking out vintage Nineties Chanel in their droves.

Stephane Cardinale - Corbis//Getty Images

And, it was not the only collection of the day that nodded right to the trends of the late 1990s and early 2000s.

Miuccia Prada's first Miu Miu collection to be presented to a real-life audience since the pandemic began was one which brought us right back to some of modern fashion's most daring moments. Remember Christina Aguilera and Britney Spears' micro mini skirts of the early Noughties? Well, these were shorter.

Estrop//Getty Images

Estrop//Getty Images

Almost every waistline in the collection was as low-slung as possible, while a number of the skirts were as mini as they could be. Teamed with tiny shirts and knits, many of the looks showed off the entire midriff, while the skirts were cut and shredded, so short that they revealed pockets underneath.

Of course, these mini skirts will not be for everyone – only the most brave of fashionistas will give them a go. But, the low-slung waistline has been reappearing time and time again in recent seasons, and Miu Miu's commitment to the style could certainly be seen as confirmation that this is more than just a passing trend.

Now all that's left is for you to decide, how low you will go?
