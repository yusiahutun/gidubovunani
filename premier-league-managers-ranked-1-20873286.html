Premier League managers ranked from 1-10 for fashion sense on the touchline

With temperatures slowly plummeting, Premier League managers across the land will be considering the best ways to combat the cold on the touchline.

Whether it’s shelling out on a new stylish grey overcoat, raiding the club shop for a standard puffer jacket or throwing on a team-coloured scarf in true Roberto Mancini fashion, top-flight bosses have some crucial decisions to make.

Remaining in-trend while wrapping up warm is not a simple task, but there are some gaffers who we reckon will be able to pull it off with ease.

Starsport have picked out the Premier League’s 10 current most-stylish managers, going by what they sport in the dugout, and ranked them on their appearance.

10. Sean Dyche (Burnley)

This one may come as a surprise to many, but unlike some of his Premier League colleagues, Dyche at least makes an effort by opting for his Burnley suit on almost every matchday.

It is sometimes accompanied by a smart black overcoat, too, which cements his place on this list of managerial fashionistas.

9. Ole Gunnar Solskjaer (Manchester United)

The Norwegian is rarely spotted on the touchline without his slick United suit on, often covered by a black coat that looks like it’s been taken straight out of the club store.

It may not be the most creative of looks, yet Solskjaer regularly looks neat and tidy at Old Trafford - which unfortunately can’t always be said of his team.

8. Ralph Hasenhuttl (Southampton)

Unlike Dyche and Solskjaer, the idea of donning a suit does not float the Saints gaffer’s boat.

He still scrubs up pretty well in the club’s fashionable tracksuit, nevertheless, and resembles a hipster PE teacher when he chucks a baseball cap on to go with it.

7. Jurgen Klopp (Liverpool)

Klopp is another member of the tracksuit gang and another boss who pulls it off, while recently opting for a red Liverpool snood to spice his look up slightly.

The German enjoys bouncing around on the touchline, whether that’s to celebrate a goal, scream at his players or protest a decision, meaning a comfortable outfit suits him best.

6. Frank Lampard (Chelsea)

When he returned to Stamford Bridge as Chelsea’s new head coach in the summer there were high expectations for the former midfielder, a cool customer in his playing days.

Lampard opted for a suit on the opening day of the season away at Man Utd, marking his Premier League managerial debut in style, but has since become accustomed to a smooth Blues tracksuit - which looks equally as suave.

5. Daniel Farke (Norwich)

The German may have come up from the Championship this season, and his team are struggling at the foot of the Premier League table, but his dress sense certainly isn't second-fiddle.

Farke regularly sports a sophisticated black polo shirt in the Norwich dugout, which is covered by one of his many stylish winter coats.

4. Mauricio Pochettino (Tottenham)

It’s been a nightmare start to the campaign for Spurs, who look to be on an alarming downward spiral in the Premier League.

The same can’t be said for Pochettino’s garments, nevertheless, as the Argentinian looks as elegant as ever this term in either a Jose Mourinho-esque blue jacket or respectable club tracksuit.

3. Marco Silva (Everton)

The Tinkerman of touchline looks, Silva sits comfortably in the elite bracket of fashionable Premier League gaffers.

Whether it’s a black shirt and suit, a classy overcoat or a tracksuit, the Portuguese is always in dapper condition when bellowing instructions at his Everton side.

2. Quique Sanchez Flores (Watford)

Silva, Pochettino and co would’ve been alerted by Sanchez Flores’ return to Watford, frantically scanning the best Italian men’s outlets in town just to keep up with the smooth Spaniard.
