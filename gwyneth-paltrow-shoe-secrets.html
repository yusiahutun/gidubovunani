Gwyneth Paltrow's Shoe Secrets - Gwyneth Paltrow's Best Shoe Moments

Christian Louboutin

Dimitrios Kambouris/ Film Magic

Stella McCartney is one of my best friends, so I was so happy to be her date to the “Alexander McQueen: Savage Beauty” gala at the Met Museum’s Costume Institute in 2011. I was lucky enough to wear some of his designs, and the night was such a great tribute to him. This is one of Stella’s gowns that have real wow factor. I paired it with Fred Leighton jewelry and super-high nude satin Louboutin platforms. The height of the shoe was empowering and flattering, which is necessary with such a high slit! I kept the shoes and rediscovered them years later when I was packing up my place in New York.

Gucci

GEORGE PIMENTAL/WIREIMAGE

This night was magical. I’m wearing Gucci, and my stylist helped me put this look together for last year’s Art + Film Gala at the Los Angeles County Museum of Art. It was an event honoring the artist Robert Irwin and the filmmaker Kathryn Bigelow. The museum is wildly inspiring for us L.A. residents, and I love taking my kids there for a cultural experience. I had also just been named CEO of my company, Goop, by our board, and stepping into that role was a major professional milestone for me. As for the dress, it was so unique—short, sexy, with an incredible neckline and sleeves. Gucci creative director Alessandro Michele is such a genius. These gold strappy heels were just the right complement.

Christian Louboutin

Mike Marsland/WIREIMAGE

Bubblegum-pink suede shoes with a pink Prada dress to match! This was a total Elizabeth Saltzman moment—she’s my stylist. I was at the National Movie Awards in London in 2010, and I’m sure that her dream was for me to look like a mix of Park Avenue lady and Catherine Deneuve: the bouffant, the eyeliner, the classic silhouette. And clearly she succeeded. I definitely held on to these Louboutins—they’re the perfect shade of pink. I have a storage area where I save a lot of the shoes I’ve worn to major events like the Grammys and the Oscars. I couldn’t even begin to tell you how many pairs I have.

Christian Louboutin

KEVIN MAZUR/WIREIMAGE

Here I am with my great friend Beyoncé at the 2011 Grammy Awards, around the time that I was guest-starring on The version of “Forget You” that I sang on the show had become a hit, and I had just performed it onstage with Cee Lo Green. Beyoncé played a huge part in helping me get through that performance alive, and you can see by my smile how relieved I was that I hadn’t totally embarrassed myself. After I sang I went back to watch Arcade Fire play, and they won Album of the Year, which was really cool. I’m wearing a sheer black Monique Lhuillier minidress with these Christian Louboutin architectural heels. Oh, man, I wish I still had them. Alas, they were loaners.

Dsquared2

ELISABETTA VILLA / GETTY IMAGES

These Dsquared2 platforms marked the beginning of a long statement-shoe phase in my life. It was 2008, and I was just starting to venture out again into the public eye after having been home with my kids for a couple of years. I’m at a press conference in Rome for with Robert Downey Jr., who is one of my favorite people, and the rest of the cast, and there were interviews, photo calls, and all the rest. I’m wearing a black Preen minidress, which seemed like an interesting choice for day at the time. I remember getting compliments on the shoes, which I archived. I try to hold on to everything so I can give them to my daughter, Apple, someday.

Christian Louboutin

PAUL FENTON/ZUMA PRESS, INC./ALAMY STOCK PHOTO

This was at a post- Oscars party after I performed a song called “Coming Home” at the Academy Awards in 2011. I was a wreck—it was the most nervous I’d ever been about performing in my life. My body wasn’t responding to my brain; I literally didn’t know how to walk from point A to point B. So before I went onstage, I chased three beta-blockers with a beer, which was way too extreme because I couldn’t feel my hands. I recall being so happy to be done with the performance portion that I couldn’t wait to get inside and have a drink with a bunch of my girlfriends. I wore a Michael Kors gown, a Jimmy Choo clutch, Forevermark earrings, and Christian Louboutin shoes—sparkly with a slight platform and pointy toes, and perfect with the dress.
