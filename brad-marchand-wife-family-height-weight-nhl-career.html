Biography of Brad Marchand

The hockey player was born and called Bradley Kevin Marchand on May 11, 1988 in Halifax, Nova Scotia, Canada. Since childhood, Brad has practiced this sport which will make him known with his father, Kevin Marchand, who was also his coach.

All of Marchand’s history was lost in his love of hockey, the sport he played most of his life. As a kid, he played minor hockey in the Nova Scotia area before playing in the QMJHL for four seasons. Without hockey, Brad said he would have ended up as a police officer.

NHL Career

Marchand made it to the National Hockey League in 2006 after being selected in that year’s draft by the Boston Bruins as the 71st overall pick. However, it wasn’t until 2009 that he made his debut against the Nashville Predators. He scored his first goal in a match against Jhonas Enroth in November 2009. He has now become a very important player for his team.

In his first full year with the team which was the 2010-2011 season, Brad recorded 21 goals. During the same season, he was voted 7th player by the supporters of the Bruins club. In 2018-19, when he played 630 total NHL games, Brad had already scored 233 goals and assisted for 250 goals.

Apart from the quality of his game and his style which can easily be described as dirty, he has become popular for his affection on the ice, which involves kissing and sometimes licking the necks and faces of other players to a point as advised by NHL administration.

On the home front, Brad Marchand represented Canada at the 2016 World Cup of Hockey, helping his team win gold and becoming the tournament’s top scorer. Before that, he played for his national junior team at the 2007 and 2008 Ice Hockey World Junior Championships before being part of the 2016 IIHF World Championships. Interestingly, Brad came back from all assignments with gold medals. The only time he earned a bronze medal was at the World Under-17 Hockey Challenge in 2005.
