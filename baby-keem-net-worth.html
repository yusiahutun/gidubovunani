Introduction

Baby Keem is a rapper who is also known as the cousin of Kendrick Lamar, the Grammy-winning American rapper.

Keem gained popularity with the release of his tracks “Orange Soda” and “Honest” in 2019, both of which are included in his second mixtape, Die for My Bitch.

He has since collaborated with renowned artists in the industry, such as his cousin Kendrick Lamar, Kanye West, and Travis Scott.

The Origin Story

Baby Keem was born on October 22, 2000, in Carson, California, as Hykeem Jamaal Carter Jr. 

He and his family eventually settled in Las Vegas, Nevada, where he spent most of his childhood.

His father, Hakeem Jamaal Carter Sr., also showed an interest in music, a pastime that runs in the Carter family.

He spent most of his youth living with his grandmother, whom he frequently mentions in his music. Her influence undoubtedly had a significant impact on him, as he later purchased a home for her.

He has alluded to having a humble upbringing.

@Getty

In a 2021 interview with Billboard, Keem revealed that he and his family had a designated day to visit Goodwill and rummage through boxes of discarded clothing for pieces to wear. He said, “I’ve seen it all, and it’s important for me to talk about that rather than trying to run away from it.”

Despite any hardships Keem endured, he persisted in achieving his goals.

Additionally, Keem has said his cousins and uncle, who were also rappers, encouraged him to pursue a career in music. However, it wasn’t until he received a modest recording setup from his grandmother when he was fifteen that he felt confident enough to begin honing his craft.

Education

Keem graduated from high school but chose to forgo university to focus on his music career.

Career

Baby Keem performs onstage during day three of Rolling Loud Miami – @Getty

Growing up in a musically inclined family, it seemed like just a matter of time before Keem explored the industry.

He spent his teenage years sending emails to music bloggers, most of which received no reply.

In the same interview with Billboard, Keem said he refused to let his unanswered messages discourage him, knowing that he eventually would break through.

Debut EP

@Getty

In November of 2017, Keem released his first EP titled Oct under his real name, Hykeem Jamaal Carter Jr.

The record consisted of nine tracks and featured a range of sounds and styles, from catchy beats to slower ballad-like songs.

Unfortunately, the EP was eventually removed from streaming services.

Keem returned two months later with his second EP titled Midnight – a nine-track record that delivered a similar style as its predecessor, only with heightened confidence.

This EP attracted more listeners, drawing slightly more attention than the first.

May of 2018 saw Keem making changes.

He released his third EP called No Name. Unlike his earlier records, No Name had only five tracks, which featured Keem singing rather than rapping. Additionally, he began releasing music under his stage name (Baby Keem) once he removed his first three EPs from streaming services in July 2018.

On July 12, 2018, Keem released Hearts and Darts. This EP featured six tracks and was produced by Cardo, who has collaborated with other famous artists, such as Travis Scott, Jay-Z, Drake, and Kanye West.

Mixtapes

Baby Keem performs during the 2023 Bonnaroo Music & Arts Festival – @Getty

Three months later, Keem released his first mixtape, The Sound of Bad Habit.

Shia LaBeouf directed a music video for the track “Gang Activities,” which garnered over a million views.

The rapper’s 2nd mixtape, Die for My Bitch, arrived on July 19, 2019, featuring some of his most popular tracks.

This mixtape included the song “Orange Soda,” which received a significant amount of attention – it received over 263.5 million on-demand streams in the United States.

In August of 2020, the song went platinum, and the music video has amassed millions of views since its release.

Debut Album

Baby Keem performs during Day 3 of Wireless Festival – @Getty

On September 20, 2021, Keem released his debut studio album, The Melodic Blue.

Keem is an executive producer on the project; he co-produced fourteen of sixteen tracks.

The album also features collaborations with Travis Scott, Kendrick Lamar, Brent Faiyaz, and Don Toliver.

“Durag Activity,” a track that Keem released as a single and features his cousin Lamar peaked at number 18 on the Billboard Hot 100.

In contrast, the second single, “Durag Activity” with Travis Scott, peaked at number 85.

Keem’s debut album generally received positive reviews from critics.

On September 9, 2021, the producer appeared on Jimmy Fallon’s The Tonight Show to perform his song “Issues,” a track from his debut album.

In addition to creating his own music and writing his own songs, Keem has also helped other artists produce their music.

He has collaborated on several projects, including Schoolboy Q’s Crash Talk, Jay Rock’s Redemption, Kendrick Lamar’s Black Panther: The Album, and the song “Nile” from Disney’s The Lion King: The Gift.

In an interview with The Rap Pack, Keem was asked whether he foresees himself producing more music for other artists in the future. Keem replied, “I’m really selective of what I do and who I work with.”

In April 2022, Keem won a Grammy for Best Rap Performance.
