The Lover's Knot tiara shares a fascinating link with the Queen's most iconic crown

The Duchess of Cambridge joined the Queen at the Dutch state banquet on Tuesday night, and both royals were dressed in treasured headpieces for the Buckingham Palace occasion.

Kate pared her silvery blue Alexander McQueen gown with elegant pearl earrings, Queen Alexandra's necklace and the Lover's Knot tiara, which she has worn on several occasions and was a favourite of Princess Diana's.

Getty Images

Kate wore the Lover’s Knot tiara to the Dutch state banquet

The Queen welcomed the King Willem-Alexander and Queen Maxima of the Netherlands wearing the Girls of Great Britain and Ireland tiara, one of the one of the most recognisable tiaras made by the London-based jeweller, House of Garrard.

Getty Images

The Queen sparked in the Girls of Great Britain and Ireland tiara

The intricate white-diamond design is recognisable from portraits of the monarch on stamps and on currency around the commonwealth, but it also shares an interesting history with the Lover's Knot. In fact, the two striking crowns are closely connected.

The Queen inherited her signature tiara from her grandmother, Queen Mary, in 1947, the year of her marriage to Prince Philip. It gets its name from the committee chaired by Lady Eva Greville, who raised money to pay for the tiara as a wedding gift to Queen Mary in 1893. Two decades later, Mary asked Garrard to tweak the headpiece which was originally topped with pearls - swapping them for diamonds. However, these precious objects didn't go to waste. Instead, they were used to create the sparkling Lover's Knot.

Getty Images

The Queen wore her signature tiara to a state banquet in 1970.

"Originally topped with pearls and being able to be worn as a necklace or small coronet, the tiara was altered in 1914 to replace the pearls with diamonds, and to remove the base so it could be worn as a separate bandeau," a spokesperson for Garrard explained to Harper's Bazaar."The pearls removed from the top of the tiara were then used in the creation of the Lover's Knot tiara, worn recently by HRH The Duchess of Cambridge."

Getty Images

Princess Diana wore the Lover’s Knot tiara to the State Opening of Parliament in 1981

Today, the Lover's Knot consists of diamonds and a collection of hanging pearls set in silver and gold. This piece was also originally commissioned by Queen Mary in 1913, before it was inherited by Queen Elizabeth in the 1950s. The Queen wore the tiara a few times, but in 1981, she passed it along to Diana. It was first worn by Kate at a Buckingham Palace reception in 2015.
