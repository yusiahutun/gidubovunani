Love Island's Will Young branded 'serial DMer' with 'dead chat' by TikTok stars

Love Island's Will Young has been branded a "serial DMer" by podcast host Grace Keeling and been accused of having "dead chat" by a TikTok star known as Kali Sluzza.

The TikTok personality appeared on the Saving Grace podcast in May last year and revealed farmer Will, who has a whopping 1.1 million followers on the social media app, was one of the well-known TikTok stars who had slid into her DMs.

Podcast host Grace, better known as GK Barry, didn't seem shocked by the admission and confessed he had also been messaging a few of her friends.

She added: "I think he's a serial DM'er. He's DM'ing one of my friends as well."

Kali responded: "Yeah but he's obviously loving it," as the podcast host chimed in: "It's because people want him for the lambs. I'd f**k him to see a lamb."

Recalling the time she once messaged him when she was drunk, Kali let slip that the 23-year-old has "dead chat."

The TikTok star revealed: "So his chat is really dead, sorry Will! But it's really boring," she said, adding: "I thought let's spice it up a bit, so I said, 'After I touch your lambs, are you going to plough me like your fields?'

"And he just goes, 'You'll have to see,' and I go, 'That's it, I'm out!',' Boring, sorry Will!"

Will, who hails from Buckinghamshire, spotted the video on TikTok and saw the funny side of the comments as he responded: "Oh behave... So did you want to see the lambs orrrrrr?"

Before heading into the Love Island villa, Will said his relationships have been difficult because he struggles to juggle them with work.

He explained: "Love Island will give me some time away from the farm to solely go out and find love. I think I'm at the time of my life where I'm mature enough to go and find a wife."

Love Island returns on January 16 at 9pm on ITV2 and ITVX.
