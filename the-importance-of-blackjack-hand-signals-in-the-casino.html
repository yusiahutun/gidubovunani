Learning the Hand Signal for ‘Hit’

The ‘Hit’ hand signals are probably among the most important to learn. Here’s how you do it: When it’s your turn and you want another card from the dealer, simply tap on the table with your fingers.

It’s a signal to the dealer that you’re not satisfied with your total and want to come closer to 21. Note that you should only use one hand for signals at the blackjack table. Stay clear and confident, and remember, practice makes perfect. So why not try it out at your next game?

Mastering the Hand Signal for ‘Stand’

This one’s all about knowing when to settle with your current hand. If you feel that your cards are good enough and you don’t want to risk going over 21 with a new one, you’ll want to ‘Stand.’

To signal this to the dealer, simply wave your hand, palm down, over your cards. But remember, it’s not just about knowing the signals, it’s about making the right decisions at the right time. Keep your cool, assess your hand carefully, and only then make the signal. With a little practice, you’ll become a pro at ‘Standing.’

Identifying the Hand Signal for ‘Double Down’

When you’re feeling confident, have a strong hand, and want to make an additional bet, you use the ‘Double Down’ signal. To do this, simply place your additional chips next to your original bet on the table, and not on top of it.

Then, lift one finger to show you want to ‘Double Down.’ Remember, this is a bold move that says you’re sure you have a winning hand. Before you make this decision, always check your cards with care. With time and practice, you’ll be doubling down like a pro! This can even be useful when playing in a casino online.

 

Implementing the Hand Signal for ‘Split’

The ‘split’ hand signal is mostly used when a player has two cards of the same value. The player places an extra bet, equal to the original, and makes a ‘V’ shape with their fingers. This indicates to the dealer that the player wants to play with two separate hands.

Be careful when using this hand signal. It’s only applicable when you have a pair of equal-valued cards. Using it at the wrong time can lead to unnecessary confusion and possible loss of your turn.

Make Full Use of Blackjack Hand Signals

Mastering the use of blackjack hand signals is crucial for any player looking to enhance their game. By observing other players’ hand signals, one can make informed decisions and increase their chances of winning.
