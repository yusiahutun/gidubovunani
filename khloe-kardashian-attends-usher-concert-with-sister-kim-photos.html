Khloe Kardashian Attends Usher Concert With Sister Kim: Photos

Living the dream! Sisters Khloé Kardashian and Kim Kardashian had the time of their lives at Usher’s Las Vegas performance.

“Alright guys, we are taking off [and] are going to the Usher concert,” the Skims mogul, 42, said in a Saturday, April 29, Instagram Story video on her private plane. “So, last weekend, my girls’ chat saw that I cheated on them and went to Usher for Chris [Appleton] and Lukas [Gage]’s wedding. I’m returning the surprise to all these lovely ladies and gentlemen and we are going to Usher!”

Kim was joined by the Good American designer, 38, Kimora Lee Simmons, Stephanie Shepherd, Tracy Romulus and other pals. While the Selfish author stunned in a black two-piece, her younger sister opted for a metallic silver dress with cutouts on her waist.

Both Kardashians stars documented their night at the show, even fangirling when Usher, 44, left the stage to join them at their seats.

“OMGGGG,” Kim captioned a Story video of Usher serenading the Baby Phat designer, 47, and feeding her a chocolate-covered strawberry. “[I’m] CRYINGGGGGGG!!!!”

While Lee Simmons — who starred in Usher’s 1997 “Nice & Slow” music video — was clearly embarrassed by the unexpected attention, the “Yeah” crooner couldn’t help but tease her. “Don’t try to act shy, Kimora,” he sang, switching up the lyrics.

Kim can then be heard in the background confessing that “she hates us” as Khloé cheered on the exchange.

The Revenge Body alum encouraged Lee Simmons to caress his toned abs during an upbeat song.

The “OMG” artist kicked off his “My Way” residency at the Dolby Live at Park MGM in February.

“I live for [the] live experience, it’s the reason why I do it,” Usher previously told Us Weekly of his Vegas shows. “The treat for me is a live audience [and] being able to have that energy with them and that connection with them. … [The stage is] the place where I get a chance to be artistic. It’s the place where I get a chance to have fun with wardrobe. It’s the place I have, you know, um, a chance to work with lights and creativity and new ideas.”
