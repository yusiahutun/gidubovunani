Top 15 cheapest stag-do destinations in Europe revealed

If you want a wild weekend without burning a hole in your pocket, listen up.

FairFX has worked out the destinations where you can get the most bang for your buck using a stag-do cost barometer.

The results revealed that Bratislava in Slovakia is the cheapest overall destination coming in at a bargain £138, including flights and hotels.

This is closely followed by Bulgaria at £156 and Lithuania at £164. Krakow in Poland comes in fourth, while beer-loving Prague in the Czech Republic was the fifth cheapest option.

*** Wild stag do ideas for thrill-seeking grooms ***

If you're after a really boozy one though, Budapest might be the place for you. You can bag yourself a pint for as little as 75p in the Hungarian capital.

And if you're after an action-packed holiday, Krakow in Poland might be best for you budget. The travel money expert, worked out that a whole day of activities would only set you back £32.

This would include costs of a beer, breakfast, dinner, a brewery tour, a paintballing session, museum visit, bus or train airport transfer, taxi ride, a 24-hour metro or tram pass, nightclub entry and a late night Big Mac

Krakow is four times cheaper than Stockholm, Sweden, which topped the rankings as the most expensive city at around £121 a day.

While demand for flights and hotels sees costs soar in places like Barcelona and Amsterdam, smart stags can save significantly by choosing lesser-known destinations.

When it comes to splashing the cash when you're there though, Krakow and Sofia are the best budget hotspots, according to the FairFX stag destination barometer.

The research found that the cheapest beer in Europe’s stag do cities could be found in Budapest from just 75p followed by Prague at 93p.

The cheapest breakfast starts at just 40p in Sofia with Krakow home to the cheapest restaurant dinner from just £2.22. Meanwhile, Dublin was home of the most expensive beer, costing upwards of £3.55, followed by Stockholm at £3.41.

Sofia also boasts the cheapest Big Mac at £1.37 and the cheapest transport with an airport transfer costing 40p, 3km taxi costing £1.61 and a 24-hour metro pass just £1.61.
