7 major rivers in Africa and their location

Do you know the major rivers in Africa? If you live in Africa or have visited the continent before, you might have noticed that rivers are flowing in every corner of the continent. The continent is home to some of the longest rivers in the world. The rivers of Africa are one of the most intriguing elements and some of the greatest attractions on the continent.

Africa has rivers that flow east, west, north and south. It is the only continent with this distinction. Some of the rivers flow through deserts while others wind their way through dense forests. Rivers in Africa are the continent's lifeblood, with millions of people and animals in the continent relying on these rivers for freshwater, food, and transportation.

List of 7 major rivers in Africa

The rivers of Africa are among the longest and most remarkable in the world. They are spectacularly scenic and add to the continent's great natural wealth. Here is a list of major rivers in Africa and their location:

1. River Nile

River Nile tops the list of the largest rivers in Africa and is arguably the world's largest river. The length of the Nile River is 6,650 km, and its total navigable area is 1,545 km. It flows northwards and passes through several countries including Ethiopia, Eritrea, Sudan, Uganda, Tanzania, Kenya, Rwanda, Burundi, Egypt, the DRC, South Sudan

It drains into the Mediterranean Sea and forms the great Nile Delta. River Nile has two major tributaries; the Blue Nile and the White Nile. The White Nile rises in the great lakes region, whereas the source of the Blue Nile is in Ethiopia. The Nile is the lifeblood of most Egyptians, and most of them live on or close to the river.

2. River Congo

Second on the list of the main rivers in Africa is River Congo. River Congo is the deepest river in the world and the ninth longest river in the world. Its length is estimated to be 4700 km. The river flows through several countries including The Democratic Republic of the Congo, Central African Republic, Angola, Republic of the Congo, Tanzania, Cameroon, Zambia, Burundi, Rwanda.

It has the largest catchment basin in Africa, covering 4.1 million square km. The river and its tributaries flow through the Congo rainforest, which is the second-largest rain forest area in the world. Its sources are in the mountains and highlands of the East African Rift. Major settlements along River Congo include Kisangani, Mbandaka, Kinshasa and the capital Brazzaville.

3. River Niger

River Niger is third on the list of famous rivers in Africa. It is the eleventh longest river in the world measuring about 4200 km. Its source is the Guinea Highlands which is located 240km from the Atlantic Ocean. It runs away from the sea into the Sahara Desert and then takes a sharp right turn near the ancient city of Timbuktu

River Niger flows through Nigeria, Mali, Niger, Algeria, Guinea, Cameroon, Burkina Faso, Cote d'Ivoire, Benin, Chad. Its major tributary is the Benue River which is itself 1400 km long. The Niger discharges into the Atlantic ocean at an area known as the Gulf of Guinea through the densely populated Niger delta.

4. Zambezi River

The Zambezi River is another biggest river in Africa. It is the largest river flowing into the Indian Ocean. Its length is estimated at 3540 km. Zambezi river flows through Zambia, Angola, Zimbabwe, Mozambique, Malawi, Tanzania, Namibia, Botswana.

Its source lies at about 1500 m above sea level, very close to the border where Zambia, Angola and the Congo meet. It is known for several waterfalls including the amazing Victoria Falls, which is one of Africa's most popular sightseeing spots. Victoria Falls is one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World.

5. River Ubangi

River Ubangi is another major river in Africa. It is the largest tributary of the Congo River. The total length of the Ubangi is approximately 2250 km. It is formed by the union of Bomu and Uele rivers. It begins at the confluence of the Mbomou and Uele Rivers and flows west, creates the border between the Central African Republic and the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

River Ubangi forms the boundary between the DRC and the Republic of Congo. It passes through The Democratic Republic of the Congo and the Central African Republic. Together with the Congo River, it provides an important transport artery for riverboats between Bangui and Brazzaville.

6. Orange River

Fifth on the list of names of rivers in Africa is River Orange. This is the largest river in South Africa with a length of 2200km. It rises in the Drakensberg Mountains in Lesotho and flows westwards through South Africa discharging its waters into the Atlantic Ocean.

The river forms a natural border between South Africa and Namibia and between South Africa and Lesotho. Its major tributary is the Vaal River. Orange River does not pass through any major cities except Upington.

7. River Kasai

Last on the list of the major rivers in the African continent. The river is also a tributary of the Congo River. River Kasai measures 2,153 km from its source on the eastern slope of the Bíe Plateau of Angola to Kwamouth.

The Kasai River originates from central Angola's highlands and stretches 1,800 km before emptying its waters into River Congo. It creates a natural border between Angola and the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

There you have it; a comprehensive list of 7 major rivers in Africa. Without its rivers, Africa would be a barren wasteland, unable to support the incredible variety of wildlife that brings visitors from around the world to enjoy wildlife safaris. If you are planning a trip to Africa, make time to visit one or more of these amazing rivers.
